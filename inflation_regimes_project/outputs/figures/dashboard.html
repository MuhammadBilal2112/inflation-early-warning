How to read this dashboard: Each country is classified into one of 6 inflation regimes discovered via Gaussian Mixture Models. The transition probability (0–100%) indicates the XGBoost model's estimated likelihood that the country will move to a higher inflation regime within the next 2 quarters. The model was trained on 1971–2014 data and tested on the COVID and Ukraine crises it never saw. Alert threshold: 30%. High risk: 50%+.
⚠ Grey countries (no data): 53 countries appear grey because the World Bank dataset lacks one or more of the three required CPI components (headline, food, energy). Notable exclusions include Australia (reports quarterly, not monthly CPI), Iran (no food CPI), Venezuela & Zimbabwe (incomplete hyperinflation-era reporting), and several conflict-affected states (Syria, Yemen, Libya, South Sudan). Gulf states such as Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, and Qatar are covered — hover over them to see their regime. Full coverage: 135 of 188 countries in the dataset.
